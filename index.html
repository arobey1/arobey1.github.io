I'm a Ph.D. student in the Department of Electrical and Systems Engineering at the University of Pennsylvania , where I am affilliated with the GRASP Lab and the Warren Center for Network and Data Sciences. I'm fortunate to be co-advised by Hamed Hassani and George J. Pappas. Previously, I completed my undergraduate studies at Swarthmore College, where I double-majored in Engineering and Mathematics.

Research interests: I'm interested in the mathematical and algorithmic foundations of robust learning. In particular, my recent work has focused on making modern tools from deep learning robust to semantic changes and distributional shifts in data. Over the course of my Ph.D., this topic has allowed me to study ideas from a wide variety of different fields, including convex optimization, control theory, generative modeling, and high-dimensional statistics.
